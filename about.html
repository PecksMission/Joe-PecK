About Me

From caregiver to patient—my story of service, struggle, and hope

Hi, I'm sharing my journey not as an expert, but as someone living through it.

My Background

I served as a medic in the military, where I learned the profound responsibility of caring for others in their most vulnerable moments. Those experiences shaped my understanding of resilience, teamwork, and the value of every life.

After my military service, I worked in a Cardiovascular Intensive Care Unit (CVICU), where I witnessed both the fragility and incredible resilience of the human body and spirit. Every day brought new challenges—heart surgeries, critical emergencies, and families waiting for news about their loved ones.

Military Medic CVICU Experience Chiari Warrior Person of Faith

When Everything Changed

Then life changed. I was diagnosed with Chiari malformation, and suddenly I went from being the caregiver to the patient. It's been a humbling journey—one that's taught me more about empathy, patience, and faith than I ever learned in my training.

The symptoms had been there for a while, but like many people with Chiari, I didn't know what I was dealing with. Headaches, dizziness, pain—things I tried to push through because that's what I'd always done. Getting the diagnosis was both a relief and the beginning of a new, uncertain chapter.

My Faith & Purpose

I'm a person of faith who attends church regularly and feels called to serve others in whatever capacity God opens up. Right now, that means raising awareness about Chiari malformation and being honest about this journey.

I don't know exactly where God is leading me, but I believe that everything—including this condition—can be used for good. If sharing my story helps even one person get diagnosed faster, feel less alone, or find hope in their own struggle, then it's worth it.

What's Next

Over time, I hope to explore different ways of sharing messages of hope, faith, and resilience. I'm interested in writing, speaking, and possibly even ministry work. This website is just the beginning—a place to start conversations and build connections.

For now, I'm focused on raising awareness about Chiari malformation and supporting others who are navigating similar challenges. Thank you for being here and for taking the time to learn about this journey.
